## Design Context

### Users
Aesthetic clinic owners and managers in Israel — typically women aged 30-55, running botox/filler/laser clinics. They deal with leads from Instagram, WhatsApp, and Google. Primary use: desktop at the clinic during the day, tracking leads and follow-ups. Secondary use: mobile while at the clinic between appointments.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **precise · warm · elevated**
Emotional goal: give the clinic owner a sense of professional control — like a premium reception desk that never forgets a client.
Tone: sophisticated competence — the kind of calm authority a luxury hotel concierge would have, not a government form.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme:** Light mode — clinic work happens in well-lit daytime settings; dark would feel clinical/cold
- **Primary color:** Warm espresso oklch(21% 0.04 40) — deep, authoritative, warm (not cold navy)
- **Highlight/CTA:** Dusty rose oklch(58% 0.13 10) — the signature aesthetic clinic color; blush is immediately understood as the domain
- **Accent:** Warm amber oklch(68% 0.1 75) — secondary actions, warmth
- **Surfaces:** Warm ivory oklch(97.5% 0.008 65) — cream-tinted, never blue-white or clinical
- **Typography:** Frank Ruhl Libre (display headings, serif authority) + Heebo (body, clean Hebrew readability)
- **Anti-references:** NOT cold blue hospital, NOT startup "AI company" purple, NOT government-green bureaucracy

### Design Principles
1. Every screen has one primary action
2. Lead status always visible — never more than one click away
3. Warm premium, never clinical or cold
4. RTL-first, Hebrew primary
5. Empty states teach the user what to add next
